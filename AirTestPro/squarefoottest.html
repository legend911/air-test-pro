Square Foot Test

This test shows how well an air purifier cleans a space per the square foot. This will be done in a variety of size rooms. The problem with most testing of this has to do with the fact that it is done in a lab with no natural objects you would have in a room. It is a great baseline test, but who knows who does the test and if the information is reliable or consistent.

My tests will be in a natural environment and you will see it within pictures and videos. Air purifiers work best in an open area and if they are constricted by objects you will get a different outcome. I will show you how these obstructions can change the efficiency of the product and how you can get the best performance without having a large air quality system in your way. I will be testing rooms from sizes 8x8x8 (width x length x height) and up.

Requirements

To do this test I have used a Dylos DC1100 particle counter. It measures large and small particles on a seperate scale that is displayed seperately. large particles are over 1 micron in size and small particles are below 0.3 microns up to 1 micron. A human hair is 10 microns just for reference.
I will be using a tape measure to get the width, length and height of a variety of real life rooms. More important than this is the fact that I will be using a real environment with a bed, couch, dressers, tables, etc... I will be testing the air purifiers in different spots within each type of room to show how much of a difference it makes to improving air quality within your home. Contrary to popular belief you can't just put it in a corner and hope that it covers a large room because it is capable of doing that in a lab environment.

Time

The testing for square footage will be done over a series of different times. I will do test for 1, 6, 12 and 24 hours. These periods of time are significant because they are the most used by air purifier systems that are auto-timers. Also people will most likely use it for these time periods. Most air purification systems claim they can clean a certain amount of particulates per square footage and I want to confirm that is true in the real world and not just in a lab.
