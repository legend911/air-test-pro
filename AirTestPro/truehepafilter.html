True HEPA Filter

One of my favorite topics is the air purification technology known as the True HEPA filter. Now you may very well know what a HEPA filter is or that it filters air better than most filters. However, you probably don't know the backstory about the "True" part of the air filters name. It all has to do with the fact that too many companies like to cheat customers by cutting down on quality and amount of product. We can see this done on just about every product sold today. You should probably look up the term "Mandatory Obsolence". Basically, companies make their products with a certain level of quality that will fail at a certain time period.

Yes you heard me right, you are being cheated and their is almost nothing you can do about it! Things were "made to last" in the old days because.... they were made to last. Now that is seen as ruining profit margins if people don't have to come back and buy more after a certain time period. This time period is guided by research on what people can "stand". This air filter is a product of quality and government regulations while (HEPA-type or HEPA-like) air filters are a product of mandatory absolence. Please look for the "True" statement and government standards that I will list below in further detail.

How It Works

The air filters are generally made of fiberglass like most air filters, but one of the best decisions made to increase particulate capture was to make the fibers randomly cross the pleated layers of the air filter. Unlike our clothing you will not see a repeated pattern of threads because this would easily allow a particulate matter to go straight through the air filter without coming into contact with anything and that is not efficient.
Another great thing is the amount of ways that dust, pollen, pet dander, etc... will get stuck within these fibers. They could become stuck to it by just making contact, they could be blown right into a fiber directly or particles could just pile up like a traffic jam and just impede other things from penetrating it. This is a good thing at first because you can catch even small stuff like gas molecules, but if too much of the air filter media ends up like this you will lose efficiency through airflow and you will need to replace it.

Specifications

The Department of Energy (DOE) created U.S. government standard that HEPA will remove 99.97% of all particles down to 0.3 micrometers (microns). If you want to know how small this is you will need a microscope because it is virtually invisible. A human hair is on average 10 microns and a dust particles ranges from 1 - 5 microns. Pollen is one of the first things we know that can be visible or invisible to the eye. However things like bacteria, paint fumes, coal smoke, smoke and carbon will not be seen by us, but it can be measured in the air and it will hurt you if inhaled in mass quantities.
