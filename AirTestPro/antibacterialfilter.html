Antibacterial Filter

The big news for air filters is usually on dust, pollen and smells that disturb you from breathing freely. The fact that it is possible to stop bacteria and viruses that travel through the air seems like a dream, but many hospitals are already using several technologies that are capable of doing this. When you go outside you don't know who you will meet or that person on the bus that is coughing without their mouth covered. Bacteria and viruses aren't just on surfaces they can travel in the air usually with the help of dust particles and other gases.

I want to make people aware that with many of these filters it will be hard to actually destroy the bacteria and viruses because of the how the air moves through these air filters. If the bacteria and viruses don't touch the surfaces of the filter media then you will find that they are not destroyed. If they do touch the surface they will be destroyed over 90% of the time. Obviously this is great, but nobody knows the average amount of bacteria that gets through these filters without being touched.

How It Works

It is very important that I tell you have antibacterial air filters will not work unless the bacteria and viruses actually touch the filter media. Within the filter you will find the components that will destroy the harmful bacteria. It will also have to stay in contact with it for at least several seconds. I know people like to think of this process as bad organisms floating around and they "POOF" they are vaporized. It doesn't quite work that way but in our perspective it is partially true.

Most antibacterial material has some amount of silver in it. It is a proven way to destroy microbials through direct contact. Like I said before this is the reason that is tough to actually do its job. When dust, pollen and other things get on the air filter they are blocking bacteria and viruses from contacting it. This is the reason I can't really promote the use of these in most home based configurations.
