HEPA Filter

If you looked at my True HEPA Air Filter webpage you probably already know what I am going to say about HEPA-like or HEPA-type air filters. They are cheaply constructed. They don't last long because they usually short you on filter media. They also aren't capable of catching particulates below 1 micron and their performance is below 90%. Pollen and dust can be below 1 micron and most other things that you breathe in and don't see are below this size. In reality this air filter does almost nothing for you.

You might be wondering why would anybody ever want an air purifier that utilizes these types of air filters. The reason is their system simply can't fit a better version in it. Also when you use a good air filter it will be thicker and that means you need more power from the fan to move the air through all the filter media. People like "quiet" whether they are in the living room watching tv or sleeping at night. Some people may even use an air purifier as a noise maker for themselves or their children. If you care about that more than your air filtration then maybe this technology is just fine for you, but if not then you should look at a "True" version.

How It Works

HEPA-like filters work just like the better types except they don't have as much material. They also may not use the same random pattern that True HEPA filters use to criss cross their fibers. This could potentially allow particulates to flow straight through the air filter and go right back into the air. Losing particulates through the filter is obviously something you don't want to happen that much if at all. Unfortunately this is what you get from going cheap.
I will say that many other air purifiers that use other technologies as the main will use a little bit of HEPA to just add the name to the list of things it has. Many manufacturers believe that to get buyers they need to list every technology under the sun. Doing everything actually has shown to be counter intuitive to the functionality of each technology.
