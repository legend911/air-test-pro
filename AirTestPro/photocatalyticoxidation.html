Photocatalytic Oxidation

IF their were ever a "cool" technology that destroyed hazardous VOCs safely it is the process of photocatalytic oxidication (PCO). Using the power of UV light and titanium dioxide it is able to destroy hazardous VOCs and leave only carbon dioxide and water in its wake. Their has a been a lot of hype on this this technique, but it is used in many commercial cleaning environments now it is has become impossible to deny. Now you are able to have it within your home.

Now the fact that the technology works is great and all, but the fact that it doesn't have any bad side effects is the best part. I still believe it is new and most of its commercial uses involve controlled areas and not open environments where people live like your home. Only time will tell if their is anything bad that comes from it, but currently it seems to be safe. If you are concerned about new technology and don't trust it you should think about using it in a less utilized space like a basement or play area until you do. Because its used for VOCs I doubt that you will be using it on a continous basis.

How It Works

The process starts with Ultraviolet light hitting the catalyst (Titanium Dioxide). This changes the structure of water molecules in the air around it to make hydroxl radicals that attack hazardous VOCs and break them up. When these VOCs are seperated at the level of an atom they make harmless carbon dioxide and water. This is the reason it is so popular as a new technology. People have finally started to realize that we should be using safer techniques to do what we need done.

The way that people use it is the real determining factor when it comes to how well this will work in your home. The oxidation system needs a little bit of space and it is best of it is given some time to make contact with all the harmful volatile organic compounds that are within the air purifier at the time. I believe if the air is recirculated within the system 3-4x per draw then it would work a lot better. Of course you could just have the air transferring fast enough to do many draws per hour.
