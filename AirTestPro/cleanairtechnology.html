Clean Air Technology

The amount of air purification technologies on the market today have risen dramatically. Each one suggests that it is the next greatest thing, but each technology is most likely only good at one function. The design of a product can lend itself to being an all-purpose product. The design of a products is truly what makes it unique to so many others. Fancy lights and touchscreens are nice, but you want something that will actually work. Many companies are just in the market to make a few extra bucks and truly haven't done the research and development needed to make a revolutionary product. finding out if their products field results are true is hard to do. I will provide you with my own testing results that are independent of any air purifier company on my testing webpage Indoor Air Quality Test.

Before you go any further, let me tell you that an air purification technology is only part of the equation. If it is used in the wrong way or poorly it can be just as bad as anything else. For example, if you put an air purifier in the corner of a room its performance and results will decrease drastically. Most air purifiers are made to gather air from all four sides and sometimes below. If it doesn't have 360 degrees of space to inhale and exhaust air and particulates you will have a marked decrease in performance. Also if the manufacturer decides not to use the correct amount of filter media you will not see the results that you expect. Many companies will just throw in a tiny amount of a technology just to say they have it. When you buy it and expect it to last a certain time you are astonished when it is all used up in a matter of days or weeks. I have seen this all to often with activated carbon filters.

In this loosely regulated marketplace it pays to have products that have been thoroughly tested by independent parties. Many companies will do testing themselves or will even pay to have them done. In both situations they have the opportunity to sway the results or the paid testers feel a sort of loyalty to their customer and will not tell the whole truth if it will not please them. Sometimes leaving certain tests out of the equation are enough to get by on results. Fortunately for you, I will be your own personal tester. I will make sure that you are given the information you need to make a great decision to improve your indoor air quality.

Technologies

True HEPA Filter

Using a high quality air filter is your best option for capturing damaging particulates like PM2.5 and PM10. It is also useful for capturing dust and pollen, which plague just about everybody in the world. However many people don't know how these filters work.

HEPA Filter

A filter within an air purification system is vital. You need something to not only capture particles, but you need something that can hold a large amount of them securely. In the beginning this air filter type was create to catch large particles, but people didn't realize just how much the smaller particles detrimented their health.

Ionic Filter

Most people who have noticed air purifiers on the market were pulled in by an add for ionic air purifiers that didn't have air filters and captures particulates silently. This all sounded good until the government alerted everybody at how dangerous using too much of this technology within a small space could be. Even today it is still used, but manufacturers have found ways to use it as a step in the process instead of the complete process.

Enter

Activated Carbon

Right after True HEPA air filters you will find activated carbon. Most people don't know what this is until I tell them its the black rocks inside the water filter in a fish tank. It removes many things from the water to keep it clean. It can do just the same with the air when it comes to smells. Gas particles become trapped in its microscopic holes and stay their forever. It is truly a great product for a green home.

Enter

Electrostatic Filter

Electricity inside of an air filter seems like a recipe for disaster, but it is quite an ingenious technology. Using static charge particles to attract particulates is great for a combined air filter combo. It is not widely used, but I think it would be better used in other applications.

Enter

Electrostatic Precipitators

You would be surprised to realize that this was first used to take particles out of aerosol and is not used in wastewater treatment. It takes particles and VOCs out of gases using an electrostatic charge.

Enter

Antibacterial Filter

Stopping bacteria can be a hard thing when you can't see it or know what it may be attaching itself too. These filters can do what they say if they bacteria actually comes into contact with it, but if other particles just end up trapping it the bacteria won't be destroyed. With that knowledge it is best just to use a True HEPA air filter.

Enter

Ultraviolet Filter

Using UV light is a great choice for destroying viruses and bacteria traveling through the air. Many hospitals use these technology in their HVAC systems and it works well. You just have to remember that the viruses have to remain in the light for more than a second to actually be destroyed. The best application uses several of these within the HVAC system and with a room air purifier it may take several intakes for that to happen.

Enter

Photocatalytic Oxidation (PCO)

One of the most interesting technologies is the process of Photocatalysis. Ultraviolet light is shined on titanium dioxide, which changes water in the air to a molecule that breaks carbon type molecules (VOCs) into less harmful molecules. It is truly a great way to destroy bacteria, viruses and VOCs.
