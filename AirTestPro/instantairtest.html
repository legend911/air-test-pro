Instant Air Test

This test is as simple as it sounds, but it is the primary test that is done for most people. We just want to know what the current particulate level is in a room. However, I will be taking tests from different areas of a room. Areas such as corners and under objects like a dresser, bed or a couch can slow down the flow of air and provide an area for dust to collect instead of being spread evenly or making it to an air purification system.

Requirements

To do this test I have used a Dylos DC1100 particle counter. It measures large and small particles on a seperate scale that is displayed seperately. large particles are over 1 micron in size and small particles are below 0.3 microns up to 1 micron. A human hair is 10 microns just for reference.
