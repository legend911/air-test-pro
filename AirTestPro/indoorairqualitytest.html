Indoor Air Quality Test

Testing products is a key to truly knowing how well it performs. We all know claims are only as good as the people stating them. In the air purifier industry their is no real enforcement on proper testing and claims. I will provide you with an independent testing source (myself). I will use my extensive Quality Assurance testing skills to provide you with the best possible data available.

Most people don't understand the level of testing that is required to fully test a product. You start with the basic tests of air purifier fan speeds and time intervals. You will then move to tests that involve the placement of the air purifier within a room. Like I have stated before, where you use your air purifier in a room will change the performance of the machine. At this point you can do tests in different rooms within your home or business. Each room is different in its own way (Humidity, entry of particulates, closed doors, common smells, sleeping habits, etc...). You will also be measuring the amount of particulates in the air and on the ground. Once particulates reach the ground an air purifier cannot pick them up. However, they hold a valuable piece of information, which is how much dust did your air purifier miss while it was on or off. Much of this has to do with environmental situations like leaving the door or window open. Many things can affect your indoor air quality and I will show you what you can expect help or hurt your situation.
