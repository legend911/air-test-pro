Electrostatic Precipitators

This technology is generally used to help big manufacturing and power plants remove particulates and gases from their exhausts. It only makes sense to make this available to the general public. The use of it within commercial air purification products is reletively new. It is good to use a technology that is a government standard in another field. Like most things you can't decisde that something will work for you because it works in some other application. Proper testing must be done to make sure this is a viable solution.

One thing that you may realize is the fact that is uses part of ionic process to function. In the Ionic Filter technology webpage I discussed that if you are able to keep the negative ions within the air purifier this becomes a better option. With precipitators this is possible and that brings a little relief to my mind, but I am sure a measurable amount is still escaping the confines of the structure. How to measure this is what will be truly important

How It Works

By super charging the gases and particles in the air they will gain a charge. With the help of this charge they are drawn to metallic plates where they are collected. These plates will need to be cleaned periodically because once they fill up they will just let these charge particles go by uncollected. It is a pretty simple process from a high level view the most important thing is how an air purifier company will design their product to utilize this technology. I believe that capturing particles after the metallic plates are full is an important matter that should function long enough to alert the user.

This probably sounds very familiar to what the old ionic air purifiers used to do when they were punished by the government for releasing too many negative ions per square foot. Negative ions take a long time (3+ days) to dissipate. These older versions didn't have very large metallic plates or have them in a great quantity. This caused them to fill up almost daily and require them to be cleaned way more often than many users wanted to clean them. Also if you don't clean them you may notice a zapping sound as if your machine has become a bug zapper. This is the static electricity being released improperly because of being clogged with particulates.
