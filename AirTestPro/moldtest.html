Mold Test

The testing for mold is almost as simple as it seems. The unfortunate thing is you have to use 3rd party test labs to actually do the hard work and pay them for the results. For many people these test will probably just show basic mold that no mycotoxins within them, but for some this could be information that could save their life. As always be very careful when coming into contact with any type of mold. Do your best not to disturb it without proper masks with air filters that are N95 rated or higher.

My tests will be in a natural environment and you will see it within pictures and videos. You will see how it is done and what the results mean and what you should look for. It can be helpful to know what the most abundant molds floating through your air is, but it is more important to know if you have any bad ones growing within your home. If you don't feel safe doing this you can hire an air quality testing professional to do the testing for you.

Requirements

You will need to purchase a mold testing kit to do these tests. They are made by several companies that work with specific test labs to complete the test. You can get these test at a home store like Lowe's or Home Depot. You can also purchase them at online retailers like Amazon. You will also need a N95 rated mask with protective glasses to help you from not ingesting unknown mold accidently. I suggest that you round all of this out with a full body suit with hood and boots as one piece. You can get this online or at your local home store for under $20. If you plan to fix the problem yourself you will need a few more things, but that will be discussed elsewhere.
